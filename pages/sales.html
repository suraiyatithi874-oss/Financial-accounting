Sales & Invoice

2.1 Retail Sales

Purpose

To manage and review point-of-sale transactions with detailed filters and actionable records - enabling efficient tracking, editing, and reporting of retail sales.

Navigation:
Menu Sales & Invoice Retail Sales
Figure 1.3: Visual Comparison of Branch Performance

How to Use

01
Configure Filters

Select any combination of dropdowns (Branch, Counter, or Date Range) to narrow down your results.

02
Execute Search

Click the SEARCH button to load relevant sales transactions into the data table.

03
Review Data

Analyze the results table including Bill No, Amount, Product, and Salesperson details.

Entry Actions

Edit Modify transaction details
View See full transaction info
Delete Remove incorrect entries
Print Generate physical/PDF bill

Retail Sales Entry

Navigation:
Menu Sales & Invoice Retail Sales New Entry

Step-by-Step Entry Process

1

Search Product

Type at least 3 characters of the product name or scan the barcode. Select the item from the smart suggestions list.

2

Enter Sale Details

Input Quantity and Discount Rate. The system automatically computes:

Total Amount Discount Amount Net Payable
3

Review Bill Information

Verify the financial breakdown before finalizing:

  • Bill No & Date
  • VAT & Delivery Charge
  • Adjust Points (Loyalty)
4

Customer & Payment

Default is Walk-in Customer. Choose Payment System (Cash/Card) and enter Given Amount to see the Change.

Finalize & Print

Enter Reference No and verify "Sales By". Click the button below to finish:

2.1 Installments Sales

Purpose

To manage and track sales made through installment plans—capturing product, customer, payment schedule, and status details for efficient follow-up and reporting.

Navigation:
Menu Sales & Invoice Installment Sales

How to Use

1

Use the filter dropdowns to narrow down installment sales based on customer, branch, status, or date range.

2

Click the Search button to load relevant installment records.

3

Review the installment sales table, which includes:

  • Bill No, Product, Customer
  • Installment Amount, Type, Package
  • Number of Installments, Last Installment Date
  • Salesperson, Branch, Status
4

Use the Action icons beside each record to:

  • Edit: Update installment details
  • View: See full transaction information
  • Delete: Remove incorrect entries
5

To add a new installment sale, click the Add New button and fill in all required fields.

Installment Sales Entry

Navigation:
Menu Sales & Invoice Installment Sales New Entry

Entry Phases

01

Basic Information

  • Bill No: Auto-generated system ID.
  • Branch: Originating outlet selection.
  • Sales Date: Transaction calendar date.
  • Employee: Select the "Sales By" staff.
02

Customer Details

Search existing records or enter new Mobile & NID details to link the credit profile.

03

Installment Setup

Package Month Frequency (Weekly/Monthly)
04

Product Entry

Add items using Serial No. System auto-calculates the periodic installment amount based on the Sales Price.

05

Financial Summary

Discounts/VAT
Processing Fee
Total PayableAuto
06

Payment & Finalize

Confirm the Paid Amount (Down payment) and Payment System.

2.3 Credit Sales

Purpose

To manage and monitor sales made on credit—tracking customer dues, product details, and payment completion status for follow-up and reconciliation.

Navigation:
Menu Sales & Invoice Credit sales

Managing Credit Sales

01
Targeted Filtering

Apply filters (Branch, Date, or Customer) to isolate specific credit records from the database.

02
Data Retrieval

Click the SEARCH button to populate the matching credit sales into the overview table.

03
Status Verification

Review the table details, paying close attention to the Status column (Complete vs. Incomplete) to track outstanding balances.

Management Actions

Edit Update credit transaction details
View See full credit history & info
Delete Remove erroneous entries

Credit Sales Entry

Navigation:
Menu Sales & Invoice Credit Sales New Entry

Entry Steps

1

Basic Setup

  • Branch: Select the branch where the sale is recorded.
  • Date: Choose the transaction date.
2

Product Entry

Search product by name or barcode (minimum 3 characters). Enter Quantity, Sale Price, and Serial No.

System auto-calculates Total
3

Bill Information

Enter Bill No (or use auto-gen). Input percentages for Discount & VAT. Add Delivery Charge & Reference No if relevant.

Total After Discount VAT Amount Total Payable
4

Customer & Payment

Search and select the Customer. Choose Payment System (e.g., Cash) and enter the Given Amount.

System calculates Due Amount (Cash)

Finalize

Select the employee in Sales By and add Remarks if needed.

2.4 Retail Sales Return

Purpose

To manage and track returned retail items—linking each return to its original sale for accurate inventory adjustment, customer service, and financial reconciliation.

Navigation:
Menu Sales & Invoice Retail Sales Return

Managing Sales Returns

01
Locate Records

Apply filters such as Date, Branch, or Bill Number to locate specific return records in the system.

02
Load Data

Click the SEARCH button to load all matching return entries into the data table.

03
Data Review

Review the table columns, including Return Bill No, Original Sales Bill No, Product details, and the Return By employee.

Return Actions

Edit Modify return details
View See full return info
Delete Remove incorrect entries

Retail Sales Return Entry

Navigation:
Menu Sales & Invoice Retail Sales Return New Entry

Entry Process

1

Sales Information Review

Confirm the original Bill No and Sale Date. Review the item details including Product Name, Quantity, Rate, Discount, and Total to ensure accuracy before processing.

2

Return Entry

Select the Product from the dropdown menu. Enter the Return Quantity, Rate, and Discount if applicable.

Auto-calculated Amount Auto-calculated Total
3

Return Details

  • Return Reason: Type a brief description for documentation.
  • Return Type: Choose the classification (e.g., Cash Return).
  • Return Payment System: Select the method (e.g., Cash).

Finalize

Carefully review the Payable Return Amount displayed on the summary.

2.5 Retail Sales Replacement

Purpose

To manage product replacements linked to original sales—ensuring accurate tracking of exchanged items, customer details, and branch-level activity for service and inventory control.

Navigation:
Menu Sales & Invoice Retail Sales Replacement

Managing Retail Sales Replacements

01
Locate Replacement Records

Apply filters (Date, Branch, or Bill No) to find specific replacement transactions in the system.

02
Load Matching Entries

Click the SEARCH button to populate the table with the relevant data.

03
Review Replacement Table

Examine the records, including Replacement Bill No, Original Sales Bill No, Product, Quantity, and the Replacement By staff member.

Transaction Actions

Edit Modify replacement details
View See full transaction info
Delete Remove incorrect entries

Retail Sales Replacement Entry

Navigation:
Menu Sales & Invoice Retail Sales Replacement New Entry

Replacement Workflow

1

Original Sales Information

Select the Branch and Customer to pull existing data. Confirm the original Bill No and Sale Date. Review the product details (Quantity, Rate, and Discount) to verify the item being replaced.

2

Return Information (Item In)

Enter the Return Bill No and Return Date. Select the product being returned and input the Quantity and Rate.

System calculates Amount & Total
3

Replacement Sale (Item Out)

Enter the Replacement Bill No and Replace Date. Search for the new Product and fill in:

  • Quantity & Rate
  • Serial/IMEI Number (if applicable)
  • Adjust Delivery/Other Charges
System calculates VAT & Adjusted Payable Amount

Payment & Finalization

Choose the Payment System and enter the Given Amount to see the Changed Amount. Select the staff in Sales By and provide a Return Reason.

Navigation:
Menu Sales & Invoice Sales Collection Credit Collection

Collection Entry Steps

1

Basic Setup

Collection Details

Auto-calculates Total Amount Auto-calculates Due Amount
