4. Stock Transfer

4.1 Requisition from Branch

Purpose

The Stock Requisition interface manages and tracks requests for transferring products between branches. It ensures proper inventory distribution, monitors workflow statuses, and maintains accurate records for auditing.

Navigation:
Menu Stock Transfer Requisition from Branch

Managing Stock Requisitions

01
Apply Filters

Refine your search by Region/Branch, Product Category, or Model. Click the SEARCH button to load the list.

02
Review Requisition Records

Scan the table for Requisition No, requesting branch, and Total Quantity. Check the current lifecycle stage in the Status column.

03
Manage Status Lifecycle

Track progress through three main stages:

  • Unapproved: Newly created, pending review.
  • Approved: Verified by the inventory team.
  • Completed: Stock has been fulfilled.

Requisition Actions

View See full details
Approve Verify and move to Approved
Delete Remove unapproved requests

4.1 Requisition Entry

Navigation:
Menu Stock Transfer Requisition from Branch New Entry

New Requisition Process

1

Requisition Source

In the Requisition From* field, choose the specific branch or outlet requesting the stock. This selection defines the destination for the inventory movement.

2

Apply Product Filters

Narrow down your product list using the dropdowns for Group, Category, Sub-Category, and Model. Click SEARCH to populate matching items.

3

Add Products & Quantities

Select the Product Name* from the table dropdown and enter the Quantity* required.

Use the + button for multiple products Totals are calculated automatically
4

Review & Remarks

Verify the TOTAL Quantity field at the bottom. Enter any notes (e.g., urgency or special instructions) in the Remarks field.

Finalize the Entry

Upon saving, the system generates a unique Requisition No (e.g., REQ000000001).

4.2 Issue to Branch

Purpose

This interface manages and records the transfer of stock items from the warehouse or Head Office (HO) to various branches. It ensures accurate tracking of products and quantities while maintaining proper documentation for auditing and branch supply management.

Navigation:
Menu Stock Transfer Issue to Branch

Managing Transfer Records

01
Refine Search with Filters

Use the dropdowns to filter by Region, Area, or Branch To. You can also narrow results by product attributes like Brand, Model, Color, or Size.

Use the search bar for specific keywords, products, or Bill Numbers.
02
Execute Search

Click the SEARCH button to load matching transfer records into the table.

03
Analyze Transfer Table

Review columns for Bill No, Requisition No, and the Total Amount. Each row confirms a supply movement from the HO/Warehouse to a branch.

Transfer Actions

View Open detailed transfer info
Edit Update transfer details
Delete Remove erroneous entries

4.2 Issue to Branch Entry

Navigation:
Menu Stock Transfer Issue to Branch New Entry

Stock Issuance Process

1

Destination Branch

In the Issue To (Branch) field, select the receiving branch or outlet. This ensures the inventory is correctly deducted from the source and added to the correct destination.

2

Link Requisition (Optional)

Select a Requisition No from the dropdown to link this transfer to an existing branch request.
3

Add Product Details

Select the Product Name and enter the Quantity for transfer. Use the Add Row (+) button for multi-product shipments.

4

Review & Remarks

Verify the TOTAL Quantity at the bottom. Use the Remarks field for notes such as carrier details, urgency, or specific instructions.

Finalize Entry

Upon clicking save, the system generates a unique Bill No (e.g., IS000006266) to track the issue.

3.3 Purchase Management

Purpose

This module helps users track, manage, and verify purchase records across all branches. It centralizes supplier transactions, product details, and payment statuses to streamline procurement and inventory workflows.

Navigation:
Menu Purchase Management Purchase

How to Use

01
Filter Purchases

Select relevant filters such as Supplier or Date Range to narrow down specific records. Click SEARCH to refresh the view.

02
Review Records

Scan the table for key details like Quantity and Amount. Use the Status column to identify pending or completed procurement cycles.

Purchase Actions

Edit Modify purchase details (Qty, Supplier)
View Open full purchase summary
Delete Remove erroneous/duplicate entries

3.3 Purchase Entry

Navigation:
Menu Purchase Management Purchase New Entry

Purchase Entry Process

1

Supplier & Purchase Info

  • Supplier: Select from dropdown (e.g., PRAN-RFL).
  • Contact: Auto-filled based on supplier selection.
  • Purchase For: Choose the destination warehouse/branch.
  • References: Input Reference No (Invoice/Chalan) and Bill No/Date.
2

Add Product Details

Select or scan the product. Enter the Quantity and confirm the MRP & Purchase Price.

Line Total: Automatically Calculated
3

Apply Discounts & VAT

Input applicable Discount (%) and VAT (%). The system performs a summary review of total quantity and amounts.

Total After Discount: System calculates final amount.
4

Handle Payment

Verify the Total Payable Amount. Enter the Paid Amount and select the Payment System (e.g., Cash).

Due Amount: Auto-calculated Balance

Finalize Entry

Submit the record or return to the previous screen.

4.3 Issue Return to Warehouse

Purpose

This interface records and manages inventory transfers from branches back to the warehouse or head office. It ensures proper tracking of quantities during returns, maintains accurate stock levels across locations, and provides documentation for auditing and reporting.

Navigation:
Menu Stock Transfer Issue Return to Warehouse

Managing Return Records

01
Apply Granular Filters

Utilize the dropdowns to filter by Branch From or specific product attributes like Color, Size, and Model. This is essential for locating specific return batches.

02
Populate Table

Click the SEARCH button. The table will display details including Bill No, Branch From, Quantity, and Total Amount.

03
Verify Row Data

Review each row to confirm the transfer transaction details. Each entry represents a formal stock reversal from a branch to the central hub.

Return Actions

View Full return summary
Edit Modify return details
Delete Remove erroneous returns

4.3 Issue Return Entry

Navigation:
Menu Stock Transfer Issue Return to Warehouse New Entry

Return Entry Process

1

Identify Source Branch

In the Branch From* field, choose the branch or outlet that is returning stock. This is critical for the system to credit the correct inventory location.

2

Product Details

Search Tip: Start typing at least 3 characters in the Product Name field to search and select items.

Enter the Quantity* to be returned. Use the (+) button to include multiple products in one bill.

3

Review Totals & Notes

Check the TOTAL Quantity field at the footer. Use the Remarks field to document the reason for the return (e.g., damaged goods, overstock, or seasonal return).

Finalize the Entry

Upon saving, the system generates an Issue Return Bill No (e.g., IR000000001).

4.4 Branch to Branch Transfer

Purpose

This interface manages and records inventory movement directly between branches or outlets. It ensures accurate tracking of products and quantities while documenting the transfer status to maintain inventory control and auditing standards.

Navigation:
Menu Stock Transfer Branch to Branch Transfer

Managing Inter-Branch Transfers

01
Apply Location Filters

Use the Branch From and Branch To dropdowns to narrow results by specific transfer routes. You can also filter by date and product attributes.

02
Review Transfer Table

Click SEARCH to view records. The table displays Transfer From/To locations and the Total Quantity shipped.

03
Monitor Receive Status
Status Tracking: Identify if a transfer is Pending (shipped but not acknowledged) or Received (confirmed by the destination branch).

Transfer Actions

View See full transfer details
Edit Update transfer data
Delete Remove erroneous entries

4.4 Branch to Branch Entry

Navigation:
Menu Stock Transfer Branch to Branch Transfer New Entry

Inter-Branch Transfer Process

1

Define Transfer Route

  • Branch From: Choose the source branch sending the stock.
  • Transfer To (Branch): Select the destination outlet receiving the items.
2

Enter Order Information

Input the Order No if this transfer is linked to a specific requisition. The system automatically generates the Bill No (e.g., TRA000000001).

The Transfer Date defaults to today but can be manually adjusted for back-dated entries.
3

Product Details

Search for products by typing at least 3 characters. Enter the Quantity for each item.

Use the + button to add multiple products

Review Totals & Save

Verify the TOTAL Quantity field. Click save to record the transaction and update stock levels across both locations.

4.5 Transfer Receive

Purpose

This interface allows branches to manage and confirm the receipt of inventory items transferred from other locations. It ensures accountability by tracking "Pending" vs "Received" statuses, maintaining accurate real-time stock levels across the entire organization.

Navigation:
Menu Stock Transfer Transfer Receive

Receiving Stock & Confirmation

01
Filter Incoming Stock

Use the Branch and Reporting Date filters to find transfers destined for your location. Click SEARCH to load the incoming list.

02
Check Receive Status

Identify the current state of each bill in the table:

  • Pending: Initiated by the sender; waiting for your confirmation.
  • Received: Already acknowledged and added to your local stock.
03
Confirm Receipt

Use the Action column to open the entry. Verifying the quantity and saving will update your branch inventory immediately.

Receive Actions

View See full transfer details
Confirm Acknowledge & receive stock
Delete Remove erroneous records
